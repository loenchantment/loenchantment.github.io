Historic Sturgeon Bay

This lovely city originally earned its name from the Menominee people who called it "Namāēw-Wīhkit" or Bay of Sturgeon due to the abundance of sturgeon. This still rings true today, you can cast a line right from one of the many parks and piers or charter a boat in this fisherman's paradise. Cultural opportunities also abound with theater and live music as well as art galleries. Museums tell the story of the many people over time who have made Sturgeon Bay their home. Come explore ...

Port Facts

Sturgeon Bay is still a vital part of shipping on the Great Lakes. As a medium sized port, it receives vessels up to 307 meters long with a carrying cpacity of over 64,000 metric tonnes. Bay Shipbuilding Company is a vital part of thie shipping industry. With a shipyard and drydock, it has operated since 1918. You can see the tug boats and barges it manufactures at work in the harbor. Lake freighters often can be seen in the dry dock where they are repaired before continuing their voyages.

Parks and Rec

Sturgeon Bay boasts over 187 acres of land set aside for your enjoyment. With over 14 parks, six tennis courts two boat launching ramps, two beaches, and an ice skating rink, you'll have trouble deciding what to do first. That doesn't even mention the waterfront walkways, you can stroll and ejoy the beautiful views. If you're looking for even more to explore, Potowatomi State Park is close by with over two miles of scenic shoreline and hiking trails.

Education

Schools in Sturgeon Bay provide high quality instruction and enrichment opportunities. Stugeon Bay High School, home of the Clippers, is rated as excellent by the Wisconsin Department of Education. As of the 2020 census, over 95% of residents have a high school degree or higher with 25.3% having a Bachelor's degree or higher.

Housing

Sturgeon Bay real estate holds its value very well. As a tourist destination, demand remains steady. The median home value is currently $220,490. With 4,516 homes and apartments in the city, you will find plenty of choices. Whether you're looking for a new apartment or a single family home, you'll find a home to suit your needs.
